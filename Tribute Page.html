Dead Poets Society

"Carpe diem — make your lives extraordinary."

About the Film

Released in 1989 and directed by Peter Weir, Dead Poets Society is a powerful drama about a group of students at the conservative Welton Academy whose lives are transformed by their unorthodox English teacher, John Keating, played by Robin Williams.

Plot Summary

Through poetry, literature, and a call to "seize the day" (Carpe diem), Mr. Keating inspires his students to think for themselves and embrace their passions. The film explores themes of individuality, conformity, and the lasting impact of a great teacher.

Legacy

Dead Poets Society won the Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay and remains one of the most beloved inspirational films of all time. Its message continues to resonate with audiences: “No matter what anybody tells you, words and ideas can change the world.”
